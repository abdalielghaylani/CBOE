Batch Information

A batch is a collection of substances that are essentially the same, but have slightly different properties.

The Batch Information section consists of these fields:

  • Scientist: The name of the scientist who created the batch.
  • Synthesis Date: The date on which the batch was created.
  • Notebook Reference: The reference to the scientistâ€™s notebook that contains the batch synthesis information.
  • Amount: Substance quantity obtained for this batch.
  • Units: Units of the amount obtained.
  • Appearance: Visual appearance of the substance comprising the batch. For example, clear yellow liquid.
  • Purity: An indication of the amount of other substances contained in the substance comprising the batch. A pure chemical substance cannot be separated into other substances by a process that does not involve any chemical reaction.
  • Purity Comments: Remarks about the degree of purity of the substance.
  • Sample ID: Identification number of each sample record.
  • Solubility: The quantity of the substance that will dissolve in a given quantity of a solvent.
  • Batch Comments: Remarks about the batch.
  • Storage Requirement Warnings: Recommendations on how the batch should be stored.
