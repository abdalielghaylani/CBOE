Editing forms

You can edit any local form or any form in the Public Forms or User Forms list. To open a form in the form editor:
1. Open the form; or, if it’s already open, select its tab.
2. In the navigation pane, click Edit Form Tab. The form is ready for you to edit.

Form elements

Form elements are objects you use to build your form. An element can hold text data, structural data, chemical formulae, and even pictures. After you build you form and use it in a query, the query results populate your form and the data appears in the form elements you use. By choosing the form elements and how to arrange them, you can view the data any way you want.
To add an element to your form, click and drag the element from the Edit Form Tab to the form. You can then bind data to the element and change its appearance.

Arranging elements

After you add elements to your form, consider moving them into a logical arrangement so that data is easy to read when you use the form. To move an element, click and drag it to the desired location.

Selecting Multiple Elements

To select more than one element at a time, SHIFT+click each element. You can then move or resize them all at the same time.

aligning elements

As you arrange multiple elements around the form, ChemBioViz displays colored rulers to help you align them.
