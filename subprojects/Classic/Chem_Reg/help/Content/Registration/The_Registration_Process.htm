The Registration Process

The registration process requires two simple steps. First, a user, the submitter (typically a chemist), enters chemical data into Registration. The data that they enter is stored in a temporary table. The submitter can search, edit, or delete the data as desired. Next, the Registrar (typically a senior chemist or lab manager) reviews the submitted data. If the Registrar decides the data is acceptable, he or she registers the data in a permanent database.

 

In registration system:

• Each record in the permanent table is assigned a unique number to identify it and to prevent duplicate records from being registered.
• Registration checks for duplicate records whenever a compound is registered in the permanent table.
