Entering Text Values

You search for records based on text data you enter and any control characters (<, >, =, etc.) you want to include. For example, to find structures that include NO2 that have a molecular weight less than 150g/mole, enter NO2 in the Formula text box and <150 in the Molweight text box. If the fields you want are not in your form, edit the form as needed or choose another form.

The search control characters, ChemBioViz recognizes are:

Exact Search (=)

Use an equal sign to search for the exact parameter that you indicate.

Example:

  • Enter "=C6H6" in the Formula field.
  • Records with only C6H6 in the formula are returned.

Less than, greater than (<, >)

Use either less than (<) or greater than(>) to limit returned values in your search results.

Example:

  • Enter <200 in the Molecular Weight field.
  • All records with a molecular weight less than 200g/mol are returned.

Range (n-m)

Example

  • Enter 200-500 in the Molecular Weight field.
  • All records with a molecular weight between 200 and 500g/mol are returned.
