Formula Search

Formula searching lets you search molecular stoichiometry, either exactly or in the range you specify. Formula queries consists of:

  • Element Symbols: Element symbols may be one or two letters. Symbols may be in upper case or lower case. Symbols may be repeated. For example, CH3CH3 is interpreted as C2H6. To avoid ambiguities:
    • Capitalize the symbols properly and insert spaces between elements
    • Use the periodic table to enter formulas
  • Element count or ranges: Element counts are single integers or ranges. Parentheses may be used to group elements and apply a count to the entire group. For example, (CH2)3 is interpreted as C3H6. Spaces or non-alphanumeric characters other than parentheses are ignored.
Formula searches are completely non-structural: CH3CH2OH matches both dimethyl ether and ethyl alcohol because both compounds have the same condensed formula: C2H6O.
